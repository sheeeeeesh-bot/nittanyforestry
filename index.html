01 — Approach

We manage forests, not just trees.

Every recommendation we make is grounded in the stand in front of us: its soils, its slope, its history of cutting, and the landowner's own goals for it—whether that's income, wildlife habitat, recreation, or simply keeping a family woodlot intact for the next generation.

  • Independent counsel—we work for the landowner, never for a mill or logging contractor.
  • Prescriptions built around Pennsylvania's oak‑hickory and northern hardwood forest types.
  • Plans written to hold up over decades, not just through the next timber sale.

02 — Services

Full-cycle forest management

From the first stand walk to the closeout of a timber sale, we handle the technical work of managing timber as an asset and an ecosystem at once.

Forest Management Plans

Ten‑year stewardship plans that inventory your timber, set management goals, and satisfy DCNR Bureau of Forestry documentation standards.

Timber Sale Administration & Marking

Paint‑marking of crop trees and boundaries, competitive bid packages, and on‑site oversight so contract terms are actually honored in the woods.

Timber Cruising & Inventory

Statistically sound stand inventories—volume, value, growth, and species composition—used to project what a stand can sustainably yield.

Boundary Line & GIS Mapping

Retracing overgrown property lines and building GPS‑based stand maps and GIS deliverables you can hand to a surveyor, lender, or heir.

Timber Stand Improvement

Crop‑tree release, invasive suppression, and regeneration surveys that account for deer browse pressure—the leading obstacle to PA forest regeneration.

Act 319 & Tax Consulting

Preparation and ongoing compliance support for Pennsylvania's Clean & Green (Act 319) preferential assessment program.

03 — Region

Rooted in the Ridge & Valley

We're based in the Nittany Valley, at the heart of Pennsylvania's Ridge‑and‑Valley Appalachian province—a landscape of long parallel oak ridges and narrow hardwood valleys that runs the length of the state.

That geography shapes every plan we write: south‑facing slopes and north‑facing coves rarely call for the same prescription, even on adjoining parcels.

Serving landowners in: Centre, Clinton, Huntingdon, Blair, Mifflin, Union, Snyder, and neighboring counties, with select work statewide.

04 — Process

How an engagement runs

  1. Stage 01

    Site walk & stand assessment

    We walk the property with you, note boundary condition, access, and your goals, and identify distinct forest stands.

  2. Stage 02

    Inventory & mapping

    A statistical cruise and GPS stand map establish what's actually growing on the property, stand by stand.

  3. Stage 03

    Written management plan

    A prescription for each stand—harvest, TSI, regeneration, or deferral—tied to your stated goals and a realistic timeline.

  4. Stage 04

    Implementation & stewardship

    Marking, sale administration, and monitoring, with the plan revisited as the forest and markets change.
